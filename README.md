E-Commerce Sales Data Analysis

Project Overview
This project involved analyzing a dataset of 3,660 e-commerce sales transactions to evaluate customer purchasing behavior, revenue performance, and category trends. By examining product categories, payment methods, and discount strategies, the analysis extracted actionable insights to guide business optimization and sales strategies.

Methodology & Data Cleaning
To ensure data integrity prior to exploratory data analysis, the following preprocessing steps were executed:

Standardized dataset column names for consistency and analytical efficiency.

Checked for duplicate values across the 3,660 records, confirming a clean dataset with zero duplicates.

Transformed the purchase date field into a standardized temporal format to enable time-series analysis.

Identified potential outliers in pricing and discounts using statistical visualizations to ensure accurate revenue modeling.

Key Findings

1. Revenue & Order Performance

Total Revenue: The platform generated a total revenue of Rs. 932,570.47 across all transactions.

Top Category by Revenue: The "Clothing" category was the highest earner, generating Rs. 139,714.55.

Top Category by Volume: The "Home & Kitchen" category led in customer engagement, recording the highest order volume at 549 orders.

Average Order Value: The average final purchase value per user was calculated to be Rs. 206.91.

Top Products: The top five highest-revenue-generating individual products contributed roughly Rs. 490 to Rs. 497 each to the total revenue.

2. Temporal Trends

Peak Sales: October 2024 recorded the highest revenue performance at Rs. 94,501.91, indicating strong seasonal demand.

Sales Troughs: November 2024 experienced a significant dip in sales, generating Rs. 64,069.64.

3. Customer Behavior & Promotions

Payment Preferences: Credit Card was the most frequently used payment method with 760 transactions, followed very closely by UPI with 757 transactions.

Discount Strategies: Analysis revealed that higher discounts do not strictly correlate with a higher total sales value. Products sold with a 0% discount contributed the most to total sales (Rs. 122,503.80), while products at a 50% discount brought in the lowest total sales (Rs. 55,258.72).

Category Discounts: The highest average discount was offered in the "Home & Kitchen" category (19.61%), while "Clothing" offered the lowest average discount (17.92%).

Strategic Recommendations
Based on the data insights, the following business recommendations were developed:

Optimize Inventory: Maintain robust stock levels for "Home & Kitchen" products to sustain the high volume of customer flow and prevent stockouts.

Targeted Marketing: Capitalize on the "Clothing" category's revenue-generating strength through targeted campaigns, cross-selling, or loyalty programs to further boost profitability.

Review Promotional Pricing: Reevaluate the discount strategy by A/B testing price points or bundling products, as deep discounts do not directly drive increased total revenue.

Support Payment Infrastructure: Ensure highly reliable and frictionless payment gateways for Credit Card and UPI, as these represent the preferred payment channels for the vast majority of the customer base.
